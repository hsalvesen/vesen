<span style="color: var(--theme-white);">The computer terminal has evolved from humble beginnings in the</span> <span style="color: var(--theme-yellow); font-weight: bold;">1960s</span><span style="color: var(--theme-white);">.</span>
<span style="color: var(--theme-white);">Originally, terminals were physical devices -- teletypewriters that</span>
<span style="color: var(--theme-white);">allowed users to interact with mainframe computers through typed commands.</span>

<span style="color: var(--theme-white);">The first video terminals appeared in the</span> <span style="color: var(--theme-yellow); font-weight: bold;">1970s</span><span style="color: var(--theme-white);"> replacing paper printouts</span>
<span style="color: var(--theme-white);">with cathode-ray tube displays. Then, with the rise of personal computers</span>
<span style="color: var(--theme-white);">in the</span> <span style="color: var(--theme-yellow); font-weight: bold;">1980s</span><span style="color: var(--theme-white);">, terminal emulators brought command-line interfaces to</span>
<span style="color: var(--theme-white);">graphical environments.</span> <span style="color: var(--theme-bright-green); font-weight: bold;">Unix</span> <span style="color: var(--theme-white);">systems</span> popularised shells like</span> <span style="color: var(--theme-bright-cyan); font-weight: bold;">sh</span><span style="color: var(--theme-white);">,</span><span style="color: var(--theme-bright-cyan); font-weight: bold;"> csh</span><span style="color: var(--theme-white);">, and</span>
<span style="color: var(--theme-white);">later <span style="color: var(--theme-bright-cyan); font-weight: bold;">bash</span><span style="color: var(--theme-white);">, making terminals powerful tools for system administration and</span>
<span style="color: var(--theme-white);">development.</span>

<span style="color: var(--theme-white);">Today's terminals blend their classic functionality with modern features</span>
<span style="color: var(--theme-white);">-- syntax highlighting, tabs, themes, and web-based implementations like</span>
<span style="color: var(--theme-white);">this one. Despite decades of GUI evolution, the terminal remains an</span>
<span style="color: var(--theme-white);">essential tool for developers, system admins, and power users worldwide.

<span style="color: var(--theme-white);">The command line endures because text is universal, scriptable, and</span>
<span style="color: var(--theme-white);">efficient, proving that sometimes the oldest interfaces are the most</span> 
<span style="color: var(--theme-bright-green); font-weight: bold;">timeless</span><span style="color: var(--theme-white);">.</span>
